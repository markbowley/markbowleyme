**Option 1 ‚Äì on a subdomain (e.g blog.website.com)** 

The subdomain route is nice and neat as a separate company blog, and is easy to set up. However, any SEO benefit does not apply to your main website. Google sees subdomains as separate sites, so the blog and front-end website would rank separately. 

Adding a Feather blog as a **s**ubdomain is easy, and can be done via the controls in your Feather dashboard.

**Option 2 ‚Äì on a subdirectory (e.g website.com/blog)**

To get the proper SEO benefit, you should use the subdirectory option. It‚Äôs a little more involved and technical to set this up, but nothing too complicated if you follow the steps.
